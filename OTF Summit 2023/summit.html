 The Autocrat in Your iPhone

(Foreign Affairs, January 2023)

The advent of advanced spyware has transformed the world of espionage and surveillance. High profile incidents involving the use of NSO Group's Pegasus technology have allowed authoritarians to spy on prominent political opposition figures, journalists, and human rights activists in many countries. The use of spyware, however, is not limited to authoritarians. Client states of NSO Group have used Pegasus against not only opposition groups and journalists, but also geopolitical rivals. Many democracies, including Spain and Mexico, have begun using spyware in ways that violate well-established norms of human rights and public accountability. This "spyware revolution" has emerged as a byproduct of a remarkable convergence of technological, social, and political developments over the past decade.
